# Ember — Positioning

> We match you to single-origin coffee the way a good friend matches you to a record — by taste, not trend.

## Who it's for

Curious drinkers who've outgrown the grocery aisle but haven't the time to chase roasters across a dozen tabs. They want *better*, told plainly. They'd rather learn one thing about Ethiopia than memorize a tasting wheel.

## Why now

Specialty coffee has never been more abundant — or more confusing. Roasters multiply; trust doesn't. People are done guessing at $22 bags. The moment belongs to whoever can turn overwhelming choice into a confident "this one's yours."

## Principles

- **Taste over jargon.** Describe the cup in words people actually use. Earn the nerdy details later.
- **Edit, don't aggregate.** A short, opinionated shelf beats an endless marketplace. We say no for you.
- **Respect the ritual.** Coffee is a daily pleasure, not a productivity hack. Warm, never breathless.

## Open questions

- Is the wedge *discovery* (find your roast) or *consistency* (never run out of the good one)?
- Do we lead with origin storytelling, or with the match itself? Which earns the subscription?
- How much taste signal do we need before the first box feels personal — three sips, or three questions?
